    Help with entry of health card information

    Health card number

    Your 10-digit health card number is found on the front of your health card.

     

    Version code

    Your alphabetic version code is found on the front of your health card. It is located to the right of your health card number.

    Note: Not all red and white health cards have a version code. If you do not see a 1 or 2-letter version code, leave the field blank.

     

    Last postal code reported to the Ministry of Health and Long-Term Care

    The Canadian Postal Code format is ANA NAN, where 'A' is a letter and 'N' is a number (e.g., K7L 3M6).

     

    Citizenship or Immigration Document

    All applications for a photo health card must show an original document that demonstrates a person's Canadian citizenship or immigration status and may be one of the following:

    • Canadian Birth Certificate

    • Canadian Passport

    • Certificate of Canadian Citizenship or Certificate of Naturalization

    • Work Permit

    • Confirmation of Permanent Residence (IMM 5292)

    • Confirmation of Permanent Residence (IMM 5688)

    • Permanent Resident Card

    • Temporary Resident Permit

    If you cannot remember which of these documents you used, you will need to change your address by visiting a ServiceOntario centre.

    Only the last 4 characters of the citizenship or immigration document are required for entry. For example, if your document number is 12345678, enter 5678 as the last 4 characters.

    Note: You may change your child’s address online if you provided a citizenship or immigration document during his/her health card registration process.

    Help with entry of driver information

    Driver’s licence number and issue date

    Your driver’s licence number is found on the front of your driver’s licence. It is 15 characters long and has a letter at the beginning, followed by a 14-digit number.

    Your driver’s licence issue date is found on the front of your driver’s licence. It begins with the 4-digit year, followed by the 2-digit month and date.

     

    Registrant identification number (RIN)

    Your 9-digit registrant identification number is found on the front of your vehicle permit.

     

    Last postal code reported to the Ministry of Transportation

    The Canadian postal code format is ANA NAN, where 'A' is a letter and 'N' is a number (e.g., K7L 3M6).
